June 19, 1861

Birth in Calamba, Laguna

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born in Calamba, Laguna, to Francisco Mercado Rizal and Teodora Alonso Realonda. He was the seventh of eleven children in a prosperous family.

Birth
Age 3
1864

First Lessons from Mother

Teodora Alonso taught José his first letters and prayers. She was his first teacher and greatly influenced his love for learning and literature.

Education
Age 9
1870

Studies in Biñan, Laguna

José was sent to study under Maestro Justiniano Aquino Cruz in Biñan. Here he learned Latin, Spanish, and other subjects, quickly becoming the brightest student.

Education
Age 11
1872

Enters Ateneo Municipal de Manila

José enrolled at Ateneo Municipal de Manila, where he excelled academically and graduated as valedictorian. He developed his artistic and literary talents here.

Education
Age 16
1877

University of Santo Tomas

José enrolled in Philosophy and Letters at UST, later shifting to Medicine. He became increasingly aware of the discrimination against Filipino students.

Education
Age 21
May 3, 1882

Journey to Europe

José secretly left the Philippines aboard the steamship Salvadora to pursue higher studies in Europe, without his parents' knowledge.

Travel
Age 21
1882-1885

Medical Studies in Madrid

José studied Medicine at Universidad Central de Madrid, earning his degree. He also studied Philosophy, Literature, and other subjects while writing for La Solidaridad.

Education
Age 25
1887

Publication of Noli Me Tangere

José published his first novel "Noli Me Tangere" in Berlin, exposing the abuses of Spanish colonial rule and the friars in the Philippines.

Writing
Age 26
August 1887

First Return to the Philippines

José returned to the Philippines but faced hostility due to his novel. He practiced medicine in Calamba and witnessed the persecution of his family.

Activism
Age 27
February 1888

Second Journey to Europe

José left for Europe again, traveling through Hong Kong and Japan. He continued his research and writing while in exile.

Travel
Age 30
1891

El Filibusterismo Published

José published his second novel "El Filibusterismo" in Ghent, Belgium, which depicted a darker and more revolutionary tone than his first novel.

Writing
Age 31
June 1892

Final Return and La Liga Filipina

José returned to the Philippines and founded La Liga Filipina, a civic organization aimed at uniting Filipinos. He was arrested and exiled to Dapitan.

Activism
Age 31-35
1892-1896

Exile in Dapitan

During his four-year exile in Dapitan, José practiced medicine, taught school, engaged in farming and business, and continued his scientific research.

Exile
Age 35
October 1896

Arrest and Imprisonment

José was arrested en route to Cuba and brought back to Manila. He was imprisoned in Fort Santiago and charged with sedition and rebellion.

Trial
Age 35
December 30, 1896

Execution at Bagumbayan

José Rizal was executed by firing squad at Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park) at 7:03 AM. His martyrdom sparked the Philippine Revolution and inspired the fight for independence.
